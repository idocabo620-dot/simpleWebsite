Beauty Industry & Cybersecurity

How makeup and skincare businesses handle ethics and protect customer data

Values Beauty Industry

Industry Overview

The beauty industry includes skincare, makeup, hair care, and fragrance brands. Many companies sell products online and use social media, loyalty programs, and customer accounts to grow their business. These companies collect customer data such as names, emails, shipping addresses, and purchase history.

Career of Interest

A career that interests me in the beauty industry is a digital marketing specialist. This job focuses on promoting products online using social media and websites, while analyzing customer behavior to improve advertising strategies.

Ethical Issue That Could Arise

An ethical issue in this career is misleading advertising, such as exaggerating product results or using heavily edited photos. This can create unrealistic expectations and pressure customers into buying products that may not work as promised.

How My Personal Values Shape My Response

My values of integrity and responsibility would guide me to promote products honestly. Respect for others means not taking advantage of customer insecurities. Continuous learning helps me stay informed about ethical marketing standards.

Cybersecurity Issues in the Beauty Industry

Beauty companies are targets for cyberattacks because they handle online payments and customer accounts. Issues may include phishing attacks, stolen login credentials, malware on checkout pages, and data leaks from third-party vendors.

Cybersecurity Best Practices

Best practices include encrypting customer data, using multi-factor authentication, securing payment systems, keeping websites updated, and training employees to recognize cyber threats.
