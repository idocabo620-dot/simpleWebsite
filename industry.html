Beauty Industry & Cybersecurity

How makeup and skincare businesses handle ethics and protect customer data

Values Beauty Industry

Industry Overview

The beauty industry includes skincare, makeup, hair care, and fragrance brands. Many beauty companies sell products online and in stores while also using social media, influencers, loyalty programs, and customer accounts to build long-term relationships. Because of online shopping and customer profiles, beauty companies often collect personal information such as names, emails, shipping addresses, and purchase history.

Career of Interest

A career that interests me in the beauty industry is a digital marketing specialist. This job focuses on promoting products online through social media, email marketing, and website content. It also involves analyzing customer behavior to understand what customers like and how to improve campaigns and sales.

Ethical Issue That Could Arise

An ethical issue that could arise in digital marketing is misleading advertising. For example, a brand might over-edit photos, hide product limitations, or exaggerate results (like promising that a skincare product will “erase acne overnight”). This could pressure customers to buy something based on unrealistic expectations.

How My Personal Values Shape My Response

My personal values guide how I would respond. With integrity, I would want marketing to be honest and clear. With respect, I would consider how ads affect people’s confidence and self-image. With responsibility, I would speak up if a campaign is misleading. With continuous learning, I would keep learning better ways to market products ethically while still meeting business goals.

Cybersecurity Issues in the Beauty Industry

Beauty companies are common targets because they process payments and store customer accounts. Some cybersecurity issues that could occur include:

  • Phishing attacks tricking employees or customers into giving passwords.
  • Credential stuffing when attackers reuse leaked passwords to access accounts.
  • Checkout skimming malware that steals card numbers during payment.
  • Fake websites pretending to be a real beauty brand to steal data.
  • Third-party vendor breaches through payment, shipping, or marketing tools.

Cybersecurity Best Practices in This Industry

Beauty companies can reduce risk by using strong cybersecurity best practices such as:

  • Encrypting customer data so it’s protected if stolen.
  • Multi-factor authentication (MFA) for employee and admin logins.
  • Secure payment processing and keeping checkout systems updated.
  • Regular patching of website software and plugins.
  • Access control so employees only see the data they truly need.
  • Security training to reduce phishing and social engineering success.
